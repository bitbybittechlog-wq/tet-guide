Part A: The Basics & Eligibility

1

Full Name & Purpose

The exam is the Teacher Eligibility Test (TET). Its purpose, rooted in the RTE Act 2009, is to establish a quality benchmark, ensuring every teacher has the essential knowledge and pedagogical skills.

2

Two Main Types

In J&K, you can take either the CTET (Central) or the JKTET (UT-level). CTET is a national exam but is officially recognized and valid for J&K government school recruitment, offering more frequent opportunities.

3

Who Needs It

It's mandatory for all aspiring teachers for government schools (Classes 1-8). A Supreme Court ruling also makes it compulsory for many in-service teachers to secure promotions or continue their service.

4

Educational Qualification

Paper I (1-5): Requires Senior Secondary (12th) with at least 50% marks + a 2-year Diploma in Elementary Education (D.El.Ed).
Paper II (6-8): Requires a Bachelor's Degree + D.El.Ed OR a Bachelor of Education (B.Ed).

5

Age Limit

There is no age limit to appear for the TET exam itself. However, age limits set by recruitment agencies (like JKSSB) are applicable when you apply for the actual teaching job after qualifying.

Part B: Exam Structure & Syllabus

6

Exam Papers

The exam has two distinct papers: Paper I for teaching Classes 1-5, and Paper II for teaching Classes 6-8. You can appear in either one or both, based on your qualifications.

7

Exam Mode

CTET is conducted Online as a Computer-Based Test (CBT). JKTET has traditionally been Offline (Pen-and-Paper OMR sheet), though you should always verify the latest notification.

8

Total Questions & Marks

Both papers consist of 150 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) for a total of 150 marks. The structure is simple and direct: one question carries one mark.

9

Exam Duration

The time allotted for each paper is 150 minutes (2.5 hours). This averages to exactly one minute per question, making time management a critical skill for success.

10

Marking Scheme

The marking scheme is straightforward and candidate-friendly: you are awarded +1 mark for every correct answer you select. There are no marks for unanswered questions.

11

Negative Marking

A major advantage: there is NO negative marking for incorrect answers. This strategic rule means you should attempt all 150 questions, even if it requires an educated guess.

12

Syllabus Overview (Paper I)

Consists of 5 sections: Child Development & Pedagogy, Language I, Language II, Mathematics, and Environmental Studies. Each section has 30 questions.

13

Syllabus Overview (Paper II)

Consists of 3 compulsory sections (Child Pedagogy, Language I & II) and a 60-mark choice between Mathematics & Science or Social Studies/Science, based on your teaching specialization.

14

Syllabus Standard

The content is based on topics from the NCERT syllabus for Classes 1 to 8. However, questions test conceptual understanding and application, not just rote memorization.

Part C: Timeline & Results

15

Exam Frequency

CTET has a reliable, predictable schedule and is held biannually (twice a year). JKTET's schedule is less regular and depends on announcements from the UT administration.

16

Qualifying Marks

General category candidates must score 60% or more (90 out of 150). Reserved categories (SC/ST/OBC) are usually given a 5% relaxation, requiring a score of 55% to qualify.

17

Certificate Validity

The TET eligibility certificate, once earned, is now valid for a lifetime. This is a significant change from the previous 7-year validity period, making it a one-time achievement.

18

Number of Attempts

There is no limit on the number of attempts. You can take the exam as many times as you wish to either qualify or to improve your score for recruitments where TET marks are given weightage.

Part D: What Happens Next

19

Eligibility, Not a Job

Qualifying the TET does not guarantee a job. It makes you eligible to apply for teaching vacancies. The actual job selection involves a separate, competitive recruitment process.

20

Time Frame for In-service Teachers

For existing teachers who must now pass the exam, the Supreme Court has mandated a two-year window. This period begins from the date the policy is officially implemented by the J&K government.
